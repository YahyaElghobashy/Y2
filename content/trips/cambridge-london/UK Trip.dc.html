YOUR HOME BASE

Cambridge

Compact, walkable, halal-rich on Mill Road, with a landmark mosque and fast trains to London. Eight hundred years of colleges along the river — give it time and it sneaks up on you.

Must-do experiences

The anchors of any Cambridge stay.

★ THE ONE TO DO
Punting on the River Cam
Glide past the Backs, King's College Chapel, the Bridge of Sighs and the Mathematical Bridge. A chauffeured 45–50 min tour is the relaxed mother–son move. Operators: Scudamore's, Let's Go Punting, Traditional Punting Co.
~£25–30 pp Book online — up to 50% cheaper
King's College ChapelMUST-SEE
£11 · the world's finest fan vaulting
Jaw-dropping Gothic architecture and the inspiration behind the mosque's timber vault. Check for Choral Evensong — sung most days in term, free and sublime.
The Backs & Mathematical BridgeFREE
The classic riverside stroll behind the colleges — the best views in the city. The Mathematical Bridge is best seen from The Granta terrace.
Fitzwilliam MuseumFREE
World-class art and antiquities — and an Egyptian collection Mama will love. Air-conditioned: a perfect heatwave refuge.
The colleges
Trinity (Wren Library & Great Court), St John's (Bridge of Sighs), Queens' (Mathematical Bridge), Emmanuel. Most charge a few pounds; some close for exams — check each college's hours.
Gt St Mary's Tower
123 steps to a 360° panorama over the colleges.
Kettle's Yard
One of England's finest 20th-c. art houses. Free.
Market Square
Daily market — produce, crafts, street food, fudge.
Jesus Green
Riverside meadows — golden-hour walks till 21:20.

ARCHITECTURAL LANDMARK · MUST-VISIT

Cambridge Central Mosque

309–313 Mill Rd, CB1 3DF · +44 1223 654020

Europe's first purpose-built eco-mosque (opened 2019), by the architects of the London Eye. Thirty tree-like timber columns hold an octagonal lattice vault echoing the fan vaulting of King's College Chapel — a deliberate fusion of Islamic and English sacred architecture. Gault-brick cladding spells Kufic calligraphy: "Say He is God, the One." RIBA Stirling Prize shortlisted, 2021.

Prayer in summer

At 52°N in late June there's persistent twilight — true night never falls, so Fajr & Isha follow convention.

HALF-DAY TRIP · 15–20 MIN BY TRAIN

Ely — the Ship of the Fens

One of England's greatest Norman cathedrals, with a unique 14th-century octagonal lantern tower — it stood in for Westminster Abbey in The Crown and The King's Speech. Add Oliver Cromwell's House, riverside walks and a 30-min Liberty Belle boat trip (~£6). Off-peak returns from ~£2.30. A peaceful, crowd-free contrast — allow ~3 hours.

Home is wherever the river bends.
ZONE BY ZONE · 48 MIN AWAY

London

Enormous, layered and food-obsessed. The trick is to cluster by area and never crisscross — and most of the great museums are free. Do London on the weekdays Mama is busy.

Getting there & around
Cambridge → London
48 min fastest (Great Northern → King's Cross); ~70 min Thameslink → St Pancras; ~80 min Greater Anglia → Liverpool St. 110+ trains/day, every seat with power & WiFi. Advance singles from ~£7–14; off-peak day returns valid after 09:30. Book via Trainline or the Greater Anglia app — and the station is a 5-min walk from the Clayton.
In London: just tap
Use contactless (card or phone) on every Tube barrier and bus — same fares as Oyster, no £10 card fee. Zone 1–2 daily cap £8.90; bus single £1.75 (cap £5.25), Hopper allows unlimited bus transfers within an hour. Tap in AND out on Tube/rail; one card per person to avoid card-clash. CityMapper for navigation.

DEEP DIVE · MUST-DO

Borough Market

8 Southwark St, SE1 1TL · London Bridge

London's oldest food market — trading on this site for ~1,000 years — with 100+ stalls across producers, specialist produce and the Borough Market Kitchen street-food area. Free entry. Come hungry.

HOURS
Closed Mon · Tue–Fri 10–17 · Sat 9–17 · Sun 10–16
BEST TIME
Weekday late morning (Tue/Wed quietest)
HALAL & UNMISSABLE
JUMA Kitchen (fully halal, Iraqi) · Arabica To Go (shawarma — confirm supplier) · seafood is naturally fine · Monmouth coffee, Bread Ahead doughnuts & the cheese stalls. It's mixed-use — halal stalls sit beside pork/alcohol, so confirm with each vendor.
Friday prayer tip: Guy's Hospital prayer room is a ~3-min walk (13:00–14:00) — pray and return within a lunch hour. Combine with Tate Modern, the Globe, the Shard & Tower Bridge, all 5–15 min away.

Shopping
For Mama
Fortnum & Mason (the most iconic food hall — tea & gift boxes), Liberty (scarves, fabrics, homeware), Selfridges. All "experience" stores worth visiting even without buying.
Worth buying here
British heritage brands, tea, vinyl & books, museum-exclusive design goods (V&A, British Museum shops are the best quality). Vintage at Portobello, Brick Lane & Camden.
Note: tax-free shopping for non-EU visitors was abolished in the UK in 2021 — no VAT refund. The return flight allows 2 checked bags for exactly this.
Evenings & after-dark
West End theatre
The quintessential London night out. TKTS booth (Leicester Sq) for same-day discounts. Jesus Christ Superstar at the Palladium, or a Globe show.
Rooftop views at sunset
Sky Garden (free, book ahead) or the Shard viewing gallery — sunset is ~21:20 in late June. Long golden light.
Thames evening cruise
From Westminster Pier past Big Ben & Tower Bridge lit up. Uber Boat by Thames Clippers is the cheap alternative.
Evening walks
South Bank from Westminster to Tower Bridge, all lit up. Or just soak up Soho's warm-evening energy.

UK is near-cashless. Wise/Revolut for good rates. Keep £20–40 cash for some stalls.
